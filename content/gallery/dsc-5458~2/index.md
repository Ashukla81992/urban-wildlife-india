The Oriental White-eye is one of those birds that rewards a second look. Small enough to be easily dismissed as 'just another small green bird', up close the lemon-yellow of the head and breast, the contrasting olive-green of the back, and above all the precise white eye-ring make it one of the most precisely patterned birds in the urban garden.

They move through gardens and parks in small, often mixed-species flocks, foraging for nectar, small insects, and soft fruit. This individual was working through a guava tree methodically, pausing briefly to look down at the lens before continuing.
