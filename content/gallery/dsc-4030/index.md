India's national bird needs little introduction — but this portrait captures something that photographs of peafowl displaying often miss: the quiet authority of a male at rest. No fan of tail feathers, just the extraordinary iridescent blue-green neck, the jewel-like crest, and an expression of absolute self-possession.

This individual was foraging through a park in late afternoon, the soft diffused light picking up every gloss and sheen on his plumage. Peafowl are increasingly common in Indian cities, roosting on water towers and building terraces as their forest habitat shrinks.
