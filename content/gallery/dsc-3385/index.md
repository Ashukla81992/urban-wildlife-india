Rhesus Macaques are India's most recognisable urban primates — found in temples, markets, railway stations, and housing colonies across the subcontinent. This juvenile had clearly acquired a mango through means best not questioned, and retreated into the palm fronds to eat it in relative peace.

The dramatic chiaroscuro lighting — deep shadow behind, warm light on the face — gives this shot an almost painterly quality. The macaque's direct gaze and delicate grip on the fruit are a reminder of how behaviourally close these animals are to us.
