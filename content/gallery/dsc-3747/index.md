The Common Tailorbird is famous for its extraordinary nest — two large leaves stitched together at the edges with plant fibre to form a cup. This pair of fledglings had recently left that nest and were perched together, still finding their feet and calling insistently for the parents that flitted just out of frame.

Tailorbirds are one of the most successful urban breeders in India, nesting in garden shrubs, hedges, and even potted plants on balconies. Their loud *tow-tow-tow* call is the soundtrack of every Indian suburb.
