Jumping spiders are among the most visually acute animals on the planet, with eight eyes arranged to give them near-360° vision — and those two large forward-facing principal eyes that give them their characteristic, almost mammalian expressiveness.

This individual had found a vantage point at the centre of a fully-open marigold and appeared to be using it as a hunting platform, watching for insects attracted to the flower. The burnt-orange of the petals and the warm texture of the stamens make this one of the most graphically striking frames in the collection.
