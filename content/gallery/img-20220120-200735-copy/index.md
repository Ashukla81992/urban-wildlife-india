Shot at close range on a darkened apartment terrace, this praying mantis delivered an expression that could only be described as confrontational. The front-facing composition — the triangular head, the forward-pointing compound eyes, the spiked forelegs raised in readiness — distils everything that makes mantises so captivating and so alien.

The Indian Praying Mantis (*Hierodula membranacea*) is one of the largest mantis species in Asia and a highly effective ambush predator, taking prey as large as small lizards and even hummingbirds-sized insects. On urban terraces at night it is drawn to the insects that gather around lights — a secondary ecological chain that runs entirely through the human-made environment.
