Few urban birds are as immediately recognisable — or as pleasing to look at — as the Red-whiskered Bulbul. The tall, pointed black crest, the red comma-shaped patch behind the eye, the clean white cheek, and the neat black-and-white patterning all combine to make this a bird that even non-birders notice and remember.

Highly vocal and sociable, it is one of the first birds to call at dawn in Indian gardens, its liquid, musical notes carrying easily over the ambient noise of a waking city.
