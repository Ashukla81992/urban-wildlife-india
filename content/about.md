Every city in India is a shared space. Leopards patrol the edge of Mumbai's housing colonies at 3 a.m. Spotted Owlets call from electricity wires in Bengaluru. Peacocks display in Gurugram's office campuses. Rhesus macaques negotiate rooftops with the confidence of long-established landlords.

Most people walk past these moments every day without a second glance.

**Urban Wildlife India** exists to change that — to document the extraordinary wild lives unfolding in the margins of our cities, and to build a community of people who look, notice, and care.

---

### The Mission

We believe that urban nature is not a lesser version of the real thing. A Shikra hunting sparrows above a bus stand is as remarkable as any wildlife you'll find in a forest reserve. The difference is only attention.

Our photographs are shot in available light, from public spaces, with zero disturbance to the animals. No baiting. No flash on owls. No feeding. The animals are doing what they always do — we're simply watching more carefully.

---

### The Work

Every image in this gallery is accompanied by the species name, location, and a story — because a photograph without context is just a pretty picture. We want you to know *what* you're looking at, *where* it lives, and *why* it matters that it's still here.

All photographs are original, shot across India's cities and urban fringes, and are protected under copyright. The watermark on every image is there for a reason.
