The Spotted Owlet is India's most urban owl — found in city parks, building cavities, old temples, and road-side trees from Kashmir to Kanyakumari. At night its sharp, screeching call is a fixture of the Indian urban soundscape, though the bird itself is rarely seen.

This shot was taken in near-darkness, the owlet's extraordinary eyes reflecting as burning orange discs against the deep blue-black sky. The eye-shine — tapetum lucidum at work — that makes the image so dramatic is the same adaptation that allows the bird to hunt in conditions that would leave a human functionally blind.
