This is the kind of image that's easy to walk past and hard to forget once you've stopped. A lone Ashy Prinia on the rim of a drainage pipe, three-quarters of the frame given over to blank grey sky, the bird pouring out its surprisingly loud song against the monsoon silence.

The Ashy Prinia is a constant in Indian cities — a small, restless warbler that colonises any patch of scrub, wasteland, or overgrown wall. In the breeding season, the male sings almost continuously from elevated perches, this drainage pipe serving just as well as any natural branch.
