The Black-winged Kite is one of India's most elegant raptors — pearl-white with black 'shoulders' and, most startlingly, blood-red eyes that give it an otherworldly quality. It is more often seen hovering over grassland or perched atop a wire than sitting on the ground, which makes this low-angle frame an unusual and intimate view.

Found in open country, farmland, and increasingly on urban fringes, it preys mainly on small rodents and large insects, hunting by hovering — a behaviour more typical of kestrels — and then dropping with closed wings.
