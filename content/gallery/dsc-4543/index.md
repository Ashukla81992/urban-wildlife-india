If you live in an Indian city and have any green space nearby, you almost certainly share it with Common Tailorbirds. They are remarkable architects — the female stitches large leaves together using plant fibre and spider silk to create a cup-shaped nest, behaviour that gives them their name.

This individual was sunning itself on a balcony railing, plumage puffed up in that round, soft way that small birds adopt when relaxed and warm. The rufous cap, a breeding-season feature in males, catches the light beautifully against the terracotta wall.
