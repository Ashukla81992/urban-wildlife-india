The Shikra is one of India's most common urban raptors — yet most people walk beneath them every day without a second glance. This individual had settled into the dappled shade of a large tree and seemed entirely unbothered, methodically working through its breast feathers with its hooked bill.

The Shikra (*Accipiter badius*) is a small sparrowhawk found across South and Southeast Asia. In cities it preys on sparrows, mynas, and small lizards — a silent, effective hunter that has adapted remarkably well to the urban landscape.
