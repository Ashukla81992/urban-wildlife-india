Few sights in urban birding match the quiet grandeur of a group of Great White Pelicans — birds with a two-metre wingspan — drifting in tight formation across a city lake. These were wintering birds, possibly from breeding colonies in Rajasthan or further north, using this urban water body as a staging ground.

The dark, reflective water acts as a perfect mirror, and the birds' white plumage takes on a warm blush from the angle of the afternoon light. Pelicans are cooperative hunters, forming lines or semicircles to herd fish into shallow water.
